Key Skill

The T.C.R.E.I. Prompt Framework

  • Task. What do you want? Be specific. Add a persona.
  • Context. Background info the AI needs.
  • References. Examples of what you like.
  • Evaluate. Check the output critically.
  • Iterate. Refine. Prompting is a conversation.

Pro Tip

Ask AI to Help You Prompt

Don't know how to write a good prompt? Ask the AI to write one for you.

Your prompt to Gemini:

“Come up with a great prompt to generate a logo for MowMakers”

Gemini's output:

“You are a professional logo designer. Create a logo for a lawn care company called MowMakers. The logo features a bold, friendly wordmark with a stylized lawn mower icon integrated into the lettering or placed above the text. The mower leaves a trail of neat, geometric grass stripes behind it, suggesting precision and craftsmanship. Color palette: deep forest green and crisp white with a warm yellow accent. Flat vector illustration style - minimal, professional, and versatile. No gradients, no drop shadows. Suitable for hats, shirts, and business cards. White background.”

Then take that prompt and paste it into Gemini's image generator, Playground, Firefly, or any other tool. Use AI to prompt AI.

Key Takeaways

What Makes a Good Logo

Here's what the best logos have in common

Design

  • Simple - works at any size, even tiny
  • Limited palette - 2-3 colors max
  • Clean typography - readable at a glance
  • Unique shape - not generic clipart

Psychology

  • Memorable - you can draw it from memory
  • Evokes emotion - trust, energy, fun
  • Tells a story - hints at what you do
  • Consistent - matches your brand tone

Practical

  • Works on light and dark backgrounds
  • Scales - favicon to billboard
  • Printable - hats, shirts, cards
  • No gradients - flat vector is safest
Step 2

What Makes a Good Logo?

Simple, memorable, and tells your story at a glance

Principles

  • Simple: Works from favicon to billboard
  • Memorable: Can you draw it from memory?
  • Relevant: Reflects your product or values
  • Versatile: Light & dark backgrounds

Common Mistakes

  • Too complex: Tiny details vanish at small sizes
  • Too many colors: Stick to 2-3 max
  • Unreadable fonts: If they can’t read it, they can’t find you
  • Generic clipart: Curate & customize AI output

Key Takeaways

What Makes a Great Webshop

The ingredients that turn visitors into customers

First Impression

  • Hero image - high quality, shows the product
  • Clear headline - what you sell in 5 words
  • Professional look - consistent colors and fonts
  • Fast load - slow = gone

Trust

  • Reviews - social proof from real people
  • About page - who's behind it?
  • Contact info - reachable = trustworthy
  • Secure checkout - SSL, known payment methods

Conversion

  • Clear CTA - one obvious button
  • Simple checkout - fewer steps = more sales
  • Pricing visible - no surprises
  • Mobile friendly - most shoppers are on phones

How to Build

Steps to Create Your Site in Claude

Use Claude to prototype fast, then refine until it looks great

  • Find a website you like. Screenshot it or grab the URL for reference
  • Prompt Claude to make yours look like it. "Make me a webshop for [your product] that looks like [reference]"
  • Upload your product photos. Drag them into the chat so Claude can use them
  • Keep tweaking until you like the look. "Make the header bigger", "Change the colors to green", "Add a testimonials section"
  • Ask Claude for feedback. "How can I improve this site? What's missing?" - then apply the suggestions
⏲ Keep building! Time is ticking.

Framework

Your 2-Minute Pitch

Follow this structure -every second counts

  • The Hook. Start with the problem. Make it relatable. (15s)
  • Your Solution. What does your product do? (20s)
  • Live Demo. Show the website on screen. (30s)
  • Why You’re Different. What’s your edge? (20s)
  • The Ask. What would you need to grow? (15s)

Shark Tank-style presentation

Pro Tips

Pitch Like a Pro

Do

  • Tell a story. People remember stories, not features
  • Show, don’t tell. Demo your website live
  • Know your numbers. Market size, target customer
  • End with energy. Strong close, clear ask

Don’t

  • Read from notes. Know your pitch by heart
  • Use jargon. Explain it like they’re 12
  • Go over time. 2 minutes means 2 minutes
  • Forget the team. Mention who does what
