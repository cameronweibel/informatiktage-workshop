Ethics

Ethical AI Usage

Important guidelines for responsible AI use in any context

General Principles

  • Ensure AI fits the task and doesn’t reinforce harmful biases
  • Obtain consent before using AI on company or school projects
  • Use secure tools and do not expose sensitive data
  • Review all AI-generated content before sharing
  • Disclose AI use to teams and clients
The Debate

AI vs. the Environment

AI is a powerful tool for sustainability, but it comes with a significant environmental footprint

AI as a Solution

  • Processes 100,000s of data points faster than humans
  • Optimizes building energy to cut CO2 by 40%
  • Maps Antarctic icebergs and tracks deforestation

AI’s Footprint

  • Data center energy to double by 2026
  • One large model = 5x a car’s lifetime emissions
  • A 15-question chat uses ~0.5L of water

Framework

The T.C.R.E.I. Framework

  • Task. What do you want the AI to do? Be specific.
  • Context. Give the background story.
  • References. Provide examples of what you like.
  • Evaluate. Check the output.
  • Iterate. Refine your prompt.

Pro Tips

Level Up Your Prompts

Techniques for when you’re not getting exactly what you want

  • Add a persona: “Act as a marketing expert…”
  • Separate into sentences: Break complex requests into shorter parts
  • Use analogies: Try “Tell me a story about how our product becomes a hero”
  • Add constraints: “One word, starts with E”
  • Go multimodal: Input images, sounds, and videos alongside text

Branding

What Makes a Good Logo?

Your logo is the face of your brand. It should be simple, memorable, and tell your story at a glance.

Principles of Great Logo Design

  • Simple: Works at any size, from favicon to billboard
  • Memorable: Can you draw it from memory?
  • Relevant: Reflects your product, industry, or values
  • Versatile: Looks good on light & dark backgrounds
  • Timeless: Avoid trendy effects that age quickly

Common Mistakes

  • Too complex: Tiny details disappear at small sizes
  • Too many colors: Stick to 2–3 colors max
  • Hard to read fonts: If people can’t read your name, they can’t find you
  • No contrast: Test on white AND dark backgrounds
  • Generic clipart: AI can help, but curate & customize the output

Design

UI/UX Quick Guide

UI = How it looks. UX = How it feels.

  • The 5-second test. Can a new visitor understand what your business is about in 5 seconds?
  • Clear calls to action. Use big, action-oriented buttons: “Shop The Collection”, “Get a Quote”
  • Consistent branding. Fonts, colors, and button styles should look the same on every page
  • Less is more. Don’t clutter pages or put buttons too close together
  • Mobile first. Most users will visit on their phones. Test it.

Framework

The AIDA Marketing Framework

A proven structure for compelling marketing content

Step 1

Attention

  • Scroll-stopping headline
  • Eye-catching image
  • Bold opening statement
Step 2

Interest

  • Relatable problem
  • Intriguing fact
  • “Did you know…?”
Step 3

Desire

  • Benefits, not features
  • “What’s in it for me?”
  • Show the transformation
Step 4

Action

  • Clear CTA
  • “Sign up” / “Shop now”
  • One thing to do next

Features vs. Benefits: “6.5″ OLED display” is a feature. “Enjoy a clearer screen in bright sunlight” is a benefit. Sell benefits.

Content Creation

Blueprint for a Great Social Media Video

The first 3 seconds are everything. No logos, no slow intros.

Video Best Practices

  • Hook immediately: “Want flawless skin?”
  • Keep it under 30 seconds
  • Add captions — users watch with sound off
  • Use trendy songs/sounds for reach
  • Use brand colors & fonts consistently

Gaming the Algorithm

  • Early engagement: Platforms boost videos popular in the first hour
  • Completion rate > watch time: Videos watched to the end rank higher
  • Make it re-watchable: Loops, puzzles, easter eggs
  • Hashtags: Use trending ones
